ISP 2022

I and two classmates (Dhakshin Parimalakumar and Trent Clatanoff) from Advanced Computer Science II collaborated on this Independent Study Project in 2022.

Summary

Our ISP in 2022 was a Twitter bot which automatically tweeted stock transactions of well-known figures in politics. A slideshow explaining the project is linked

here

Concept

We made a Twitter bot which reports live stock trades from a list of 25 congressional representatives who stock traders commonly believe perform "Insider Trading." "Insider trading" is the buying or sellingof a company's stocks or other securities by individuals with access to confidential information relating to the company. Essentially, politicians who can pass legislation could potentially affect the economic performance of a corporation, resulting in a change in its stock price. By having some control over the stock price of a company, one of these politicians could shift the market in favor of the stocks they own, creating an unfair andvantage for themselves.

Motivation

  • Exposure
    • Insider trading is a well-known problem within the politics today. However, many aspiring stock traders don't know where to find the stock holdings of those who may be involved in insider trading, despite the availability of these records online.
    • Through this bot, we spread this valuable information onto a platform that could be reached by those less knowledgeable in the stock market. As a result, we helped amateur traders benefit equally from the policy decisions made by politicians "in the know" and shined light on potentially illegal and immoral actions by those in power.
  • Financial Assistance
    • Although our main focus in this project was in revealing insider trading, we also understood that knowing the stock trades of non-insider traders could be useful. For beginner investors, simply seeing what stocks successful traders bought and sold could be a useful starting point.

Final Thoughts (Expected and Unexpected Aspects of Development)

Our coding using BeautifulSoup went as expected. This was because BeautifulSoup is a very intuitive Python library and we had already studied it before starting to write the code for our Twitter bot.

Some unexpected aspects of development, on the other hand, included initially connecting to Twitter and managing the rate at which our bot tweeted.

Our first main challenge was setting up our developer account. On Twitter, there is a standard process for creating a bot account, which includes marking your account as a bot account to avoid confusion as to whether accounts are bots or real people. Also, getting our api key to match the authenticator and connecting our account to the Tweepy API was more difficult than we had anticipated.

Also, we initially forgot to limit the number of Tweets our bot could create. We didn't tell the bot to stop Tweeting once it hit its rate limit, so it tweeted every available data point from our database. As a result, Twitter temporary flagged our account for spam.
